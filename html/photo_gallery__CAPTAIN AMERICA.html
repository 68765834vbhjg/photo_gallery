Captain America

10 Lessons To Learn from Captain America

  1. Don't Let Our Emotions Get The Best of Us

    2. The fight between Steve, Tony, and Bucky at the end of Captain America: Civil War is one of the most intensely emotional fights in the franchise. Steve is clearly worried about saving Bucky, and given that Tony is so intent on killing him, this makes sense. However, it was still difficult to see how hard he went after Tony at the very end, and this is a reminder for us all to not let our emotions get the best of us even in a crisis.

  2. Self-sacrifice isn't always a virtue

    3. One of the values that sets Steve Rogers as a character apart is his willingness to sacrifice himself to save others. He shows this right away when he's willing to throw himself on the grenade and then again when he flies the plane into the ice. While self-sacrifice is a good thing, and it's at the core of the character in many ways, he also reminds us all that it's okay to fight for and take care of yourself, too.

  3. Let Things Go

    4. One thing that many fans were frustrated about in regards to the romantic arc given to Captain America in the films was how he seemed to never really get over Peggy. While this might have made sense if they had really dated, they never shared more than a kiss and the promise of something in the future. So, his hang-up over Peggy reminds us all to not get too hung up over our exes.

  4. Trust People

    5. One thing that many fans were frustrated about in regards to the romantic arc given to Captain America in the films was how he seemed to never really get over Peggy. While this might have made sense if they had really dated, they never shared more than a kiss and the promise of something in the future. So, his hang-up over Peggy reminds us all to not get too hung up over our exes.

  5. Don't Put Too Much Trust In Orders

    6. On the other hand, while learning to trust people and being open to that is important, Steve Roger’s journey also reminds us that putting too much trust in authority and systems can lead to trouble. Steve is understandably pretty crushed when he learns that S.H.I.E.L.D. has been infiltrated by HYDRA all along, and it's a good reminder to us all to question the systems we are all a part of.

  6. Communicate!

    7. A big part of the reason why the Avengers break apart and the fight between Steve and Tony happens is because they aren't able to really sit down together and work things out. Tony is also at fault here, but the fact that neither of them can just come to the table to communicate effectively leads to misunderstandings, anger, and hurt feelings. Learning how to communicate with people in our lives whether personally or professionally is key.

  7. That stubbornness can be good but only to an extent

    8. One of Captain America's other most defining traits is that he's extremely stubborn. This stubbornness is what allows him to be so heroic in many ways, and his catchphrase is even “I can do this all day” which speaks to never giving up. While being stubborn has a time and place, there are also times where it's important to know when you're wrong and stand down.

  8. Feel Your Feelings

    9. Steve is a kind person, but he's also rather private in many ways. He often bottles his feelings up and doesn't actually tell those closest to him what he's going through. He didn't tell many people around him about Bucky, and he also kept information about Tony's parents from him which led to the big fight. These mistakes help us remember that being open and honest is essential.

  9. Talk To Your Friends

    10. h Fans were understandably confused and also upset when Steve suddenly went to the past and no conversations about it were shown. While filmmakers said that Steve and Bucky talked about this off-screen, the fact that Steve didn't tell Sam is troubling. This mistake is there to tell us all that it's important to be honest and open with your friends.

  10. Live Life In The Present

    11. While some fans thought this was a great ending for the character, others were upset at this narrative choice. It seemed to go against everything the character stood for. While missing people from the past is understandable, no one should live in the past. While most people can't actually get the chance at a time-traveling do-over, this mistake shows us that we shouldn't spend all of our time dwelling on the past and what could have been.
